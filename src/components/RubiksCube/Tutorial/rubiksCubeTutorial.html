Introduction

In this tutorial I want to work intuitive without notations. The first two layer works pretty well with this method, but with the last layer you can't go around.


Notations


L
R
U
D
F
L'
R'
U'
D
F'
This are the only notations you need in this guide. As you might guessed, the letter alone stands for the direction clockwise and letter with ' is counterclockwise. For example, L is left clockwise and L' is left counterclockwise. You have to look at it from the from perspective.

Cross

The first thing to do is the cross. It's pretty easy and straight forward. Start with the white side on top. The easiest thing is to put the white piece under the slot where it belongs to and then make a simple turn to put it on top. This step can be easily solved intuitive and algorithms or certain notations are not neceary.


After that step, the cube should look like this.


First Layer

After you finished the cross on the top, you need to put all the corners in. The simplest way is to put it under the spot where it belongs to while the white color is facing you, und make a simple D or D' move so you cannot use the L or R spot without getting the corner in a different position. In this example the white one is facing towards you. But you can adapt all the moves from it because all the steps for the other cases are in this algorithm implemented.


After that step, the cube should look like this.


Second Layer

For this step it is easier when you turn your cube around so that the yellow side is in top and the white on the bottom. To have a better overview. What easy at this step is, put the edge on the top so that the color on the front will match. Then turn the top layer away from the edge, bring the side up and bring the top layer back. Now turn it with L/R' back in the right spot.

The first two layer is more intuitive than notations and algorithm. So it is not that hard. Try a little bit for yourself and get comfortable with the cube and how it turns and look whats happening with the other stones.


After that step, the cube should look like this.


Yellow Cross

Now all the notations and algorithm comes step by step. That step is one of the easiest ones and you only need to know one algorithm. There are 4 different states that are important how the yellow top could look like. I know there are many more shapes that can be on top but these are the ones you are looking for.

Hold the cube so that you can see one of the patterns above. If you have only the dot, it doesn't matter how you hold the cube. For the mirrored L shape hold is so that it is '9 o'clock'. The straight line should go from left to right and when you have the cross just skip this section because you are already done :-)
Always use: F - R - U - R' - U' - F'

Dot is step 0 - 6
Mirrored L is step 7 - 13
Straight Line is step 14 - 19
The algorithm for this section is always the same, no matter how your yellow top is looking like.

After that step, the cube should look like this.


Orient Cross

At this section there you can't solve the cube by intuition anymore. You can see that the cross is solved but it doesn't match with the colors on the side.
Turn the upper layer around and look what color match with the others. Your goal is to find a pair that is either oposite or at 3 o'clock. You can only have those 2 states. You can have either solved all four than skip this step, or second you have two colors matching with the colors from the first two layers.
Front and Side is matching
Turn the cube so that the matching colors are pointing back and to the right Like when the time is 3 o'clock. Then do a this simple algorithm:
R-U-R'-U-R-U2-R'
and at the end a simple U turn to line them all up.
You dont care how you hold the cube. You can do it from what side you want. Now in this example when you turn the cube, it's personal preferance if you want so you see that the colors blue and green and red and orange are now opposite of each other.
In the next step you can see what to do when the opposite colors are matching.
After that step, the cube should look like this.


Orient Edges

At this section you can have solved either none, one or all. When you have none, it doesn't matter how you hold your cube. Your goal is to find one corner that is in it's right spot and turn this to the front side on the right.
Do this algorithm
U - R - U' - L' - U - R' - U' - L
Now you see that one of the four edges is on the right spot but not in its correct place. Turn the cube so, that the right edge is on the front right spot.
Do the same algorithm again to line up all the other edges.

U - R - U' - L' - U - R' - U' - L


Now all of the edges are now correct oriented and the cube should look like this.


Last Step

In this last step you have get every corner in the right place in the right position. Hold the cube so that the corner you want to solve is in the front to your right. Now to get the corner in the right position do a simple algorithm as long as the yellow color is facing upwards:

R' - D' - R - D
For now you should not care about the destroyed cube. When this step is done, and you did everything correctly the whole cube should be finished. When you done the corner, make a U turn to put the next corner in the front right position and do the same algorithm as before. You should do this with every corner until the cube is solves. At last align the layers correct.

After that step, the cube is finished.
Congratulations, your cube is now solved :-)
